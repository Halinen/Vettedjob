After completing my undergraduate studies in electrical engineering, I co-founded a
company focused on the development of microwave plasma CVD systems for diamond
synthesis, serving as Chief Technology Officer for five years. I was responsible for
the design and optimisation of microwave resonant cavities, plasma reactors, and
vacuum systems, approaching every subsystem from fundamental physical principles.
Through iterative experimental development, we achieved stable and reproducible
plasma operation at 10\,kW power levels and translated this into small-series
industrial production of approximately twenty systems. This experience gave me a
deep understanding of plasma--material interactions, the relationship between process
parameters and material structure, and the practical constraints involved in
translating physical understanding into reliable, controlled experimental systems.
The work resulted in five granted patents, of which I am the primary inventor on all.

\vspace{0.3cm}

Motivated by a desire to build stronger theoretical foundations in plasma physics and
materials science, I pursued a master's degree at KTH in the Plasma Track of the
Electromagnetics, Fusion and Space Engineering programme. I am currently conducting
my thesis research at Ångström Laboratory in collaboration with the Tandem
Laboratory, investigating irradiation-induced degradation mechanisms in protective
Cr--Nb--N coatings on zirconium substrates. My work involves independently planning
and executing ion irradiation experiments at the Tandem accelerator facility, combined
with depth-resolved characterisation using ToF-ERDA, GI-XRD, SEM, and
nanoindentation. A key aspect of my approach has been carefully aligning
SRIM-predicted damage depth profiles with the effective probe depths of each
analytical technique to ensure quantitatively reliable interpretation of near-surface
modifications. This work has strengthened my interest in [specific connection to
target position, e.g.\ ion--solid interactions / functional coatings / plasma-assisted
synthesis / surface and interface phenomena].
